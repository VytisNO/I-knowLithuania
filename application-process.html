Application Process

How to apply for studies in Lithuania

← Back to Studies

Main Admission Routes

International students usually apply through one of the following options:

  • Apply.studies.lt – Centralized online application platform for many Lithuanian universities.
  • Direct application via the official website of the selected university.

It's recommended to start the application process early, even before deadlines open, to prepare documents and get advice.

Key Requirements

Requirements vary by program and level (Bachelor, Master, PhD), but generally include:

  • High school diploma (Bachelor) / Bachelor's degree (Master)
  • Proof of English proficiency (IELTS 5.5+, TOEFL, or equivalent certificate)
  • Passport or national ID copy
  • Motivation letter and CV
  • Letters of recommendation (usually for Master’s and PhD)
  • Portfolio (for creative or design-related studies)

Admission Timeline & Deadlines

Deadlines depend on the university and program, but a typical timeline looks like:

  • January–March: Research programs and prepare documents
  • April–July: Submit application (autumn intake)
  • July–August: Interviews, additional exams (if applicable)
  • August–September: Final admission, visa/residence permit process

Some universities also offer a spring intake (starting in February), with deadlines in October–December.

Application Fees

Some universities require a non-refundable application fee (usually between €50–€100). This is used to process and evaluate your documents.

After Acceptance

If accepted, the university will issue an acceptance letter, which is needed to:

  • Apply for a student visa or temporary residence permit
  • Arrange accommodation and health insurance
  • Pay the tuition fee (first semester or full year)
