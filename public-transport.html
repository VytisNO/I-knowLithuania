Public Transport

Getting around cities and towns in Lithuania

← Back to Life

City Transport

Most cities use buses and trolleybuses. Public transport is reliable and affordable, especially with student or resident discounts.

  • Single ticket: €0.65–€1.00 (cheaper with e-ticket or app)
  • Monthly pass: from €29 (students pay less)
  • Discounts: available for students, seniors, and registered residents

Ticketing & Apps

Most cities support electronic ticketing via mobile apps or top-up cards:

  • Trafi – real-time planner and e-ticket app (Vilnius)
  • m.Ticket – mobile ticket platform for multiple cities
  • JUDU (Vilnius Card) – for buying and managing Vilnius travel cards

Night & Weekend Service

Major cities offer reduced night routes (typically hourly) and extended schedules on weekends. Check local apps for details.

Intercity Travel

Trains and buses connect all major cities and towns. Travel times:

  • Vilnius → Kaunas: ~1.5 hrs by train or express bus
  • Vilnius → Klaipėda: ~4 hrs by train

Useful platforms:

  • Lithuanian Railways
  • Bus tickets

Micromobility: Bikes & Scooters

In cities like Vilnius and Kaunas, you can use rental scooters or shared bicycles:

  • Bolt – scooters, bikes, and ride-hailing
  • CityBee – cars, vans, and electric bikes
  • JUDU Bike Sharing (Vilnius)
