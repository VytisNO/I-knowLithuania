Who Needs a Work Permit?

Non-EU/EEA citizens are generally required to obtain a work permit or a residence permit based on employment to work legally in Lithuania.

EU/EEA citizens and Swiss nationals can work in Lithuania without a permit but must register their stay if staying over 90 days.

Types of Work Permits

  • Temporary Work Permit: Issued for up to 1 year, usually tied to one employer.
  • EU Blue Card: For highly skilled workers, valid up to 3 years, allows mobility within the EU.
  • Startup Visa: For non-EU founders of innovative businesses, allowing them to develop their startup in Lithuania.
  • Intra-Company Transfer: For employees of international companies transferred to Lithuanian branches.

Application Process

  1. Find an employer in Lithuania willing to sponsor you.
  2. The employer applies to the Lithuanian Employment Service or Migration Department.
  3. Submit your personal documents (passport, photo, qualifications).
  4. Wait for a decision (usually 1–2 months).
  5. Receive your permit and register your place of residence.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport
  • Employment contract or job offer
  • Proof of qualifications (e.g., degree certificates)
  • Proof of accommodation in Lithuania
  • Health insurance (can be purchased locally)
  • Application fee payment receipt

Processing Time & Fees

Typical processing time is 1–2 months. Faster processing is available for an additional fee.

Fees vary from €60 to €120 depending on permit type and urgency.

After Arrival

Once you arrive, you must register your place of residence at the local migration office and may be issued a residence card if needed.

Many foreigners choose to take basic Lithuanian language courses to ease integration into society and the workplace.
