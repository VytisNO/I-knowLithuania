Overview

Lithuania's economy is expanding, and so is its demand for professionals. Major sectors include IT, logistics, manufacturing, fintech, and life sciences.

Popular Job Platforms

Work Permits for Foreigners

Non-EU citizens typically need a work permit or residence permit based on employment. Employers usually initiate the process through the Lithuanian Employment Service or Migration Department.

EU/EEA citizens do not need a work permit but must register their stay after 90 days.

Average Salaries

Salaries vary by industry and experience. In 2025, the average gross monthly salary in Lithuania is around €2,000.

  • IT professionals: €2,500–€4,000+
  • Manufacturing: €1,200–€1,800
  • Finance: €1,800–€3,000
  • Customer Support: €1,000–€1,500

Workplace Culture

Lithuanian workplaces emphasize punctuality, professionalism, and efficiency. English is widely used in international companies, while Lithuanian is still dominant in local businesses.

Work–life balance is becoming more important, with remote work and flexible hours increasingly offered.

Support for Foreign Workers

Organizations like Work in Lithuania provide guidance on relocation, legal procedures, and integration. Language courses, housing support, and relocation guides are also available.
